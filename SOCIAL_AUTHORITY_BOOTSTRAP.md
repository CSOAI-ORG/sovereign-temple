## The Narrative Arc

MEOKCLAW is not just software. It is a **political statement** about who controls AI.

**The Story:**
> "In 2026, four companies control 90% of AI inference. Every question you ask, every thought you express, flows through their servers, their guardrails, their politics. MEOKCLAW is the alternative: your AI runs on your hardware, your models vote on every answer, and no single vendor can censor or manipulate your outputs. Sovereignty is the default."

This narrative resonates with:
- **Privacy advocates** (Snowden-era paranoia, legit)
- **Regulated industries** (healthcare, finance, legal)
- **Global South** (avoids US-centric AI bias)
- **Open source developers** (MIT license, no lock-in)
- **Researchers** (reproducible, auditable AI)

## The Viral Mechanics

### 1. "Deploy on a Pi" Challenge

Raspberry Pi 5 costs $80. If MEOKCLAW runs a full council mode on it (even if slow), that's viral gold.

**Tweet format:**
> "$80 Raspberry Pi 5 running 12 AI models in council mode. Total cost: $80 one-time. Cloud equivalent: $200/month. Sovereignty is affordable."

### 2. "Cloud Bill Shaming"

Monthly thread comparing a user's OpenAI bill to their MEOKCLAW setup cost.

> "Last month I spent $847 on OpenAI API calls. This month I spent $0 — same workload, running on a $600 used Mac Mini. Here's how..."

### 3. "Censorship Resistance" Demo

Show MEOKCLAW answering questions that cloud APIs refuse, not because the content is harmful, but because the topic is politically sensitive.

> "When you own the models, you own the answers."

### 4. "12 Models Agree" Meme Format

Visual format showing 12 model avatars voting, with the consensus answer highlighted.

> "12 models deliberated. 11 agreed. 1 dissent (noted). The answer is..."

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Don't over-hype AGI** — Stay grounded in practical benefits (cost, privacy, reliability)
2. **Don't attack competitors directly** — Let benchmarks speak; never name-call OpenAI/Anthropic
3. **Don't gatekeep the community** — Be welcoming to beginners, not just ML engineers
4. **Don't ignore criticism** — Engage with valid concerns about council mode latency
5. **Don't neglect documentation** — Bad docs kill adoption faster than bad code

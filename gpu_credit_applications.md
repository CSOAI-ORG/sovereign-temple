**Company:** MEOK AI LABS
**Founder:** Nick Templeman
**Location:** United Kingdom
**Stage:** Pre-seed, bootstrapped, 100% founder-owned

### What we're building

MEOK AI LABS is building sovereign AI infrastructure — voice AI assistants with care-aligned neural networks, persistent memory, and transparent governance. Our flagship system runs dual-personality voice AI (Jarvis and Sophie) powered by 9 trained neural models, 171 MCP tools, and a knowledge base spanning 40 civilisational traditions. This is not a pitch deck. It is working software, running today, with over 1,400 episodic memories accumulated through real use.

### The problem

Current AI assistants are stateless, opaque, and aligned to platform incentives rather than user wellbeing. They forget everything between sessions, offer no transparency into their reasoning, and concentrate control in the hands of a few large providers. Users deserve AI that remembers them, explains itself, and operates under governance they can inspect.

### Our approach

We have built a sovereign AI architecture from first principles:

- **Care-aligned neural networks:** 9 PyTorch models trained on conversation data, including emotional intelligence, ethical reasoning, and relationship evolution networks. These are not prompt hacks — they are trained weights that shape every response.
- **Byzantine consensus governance:** A 13-member council of specialised AI agents reaches consensus on significant decisions, providing auditable, multi-perspective reasoning.
- **Persistent episodic memory:** Over 1,400 episodes stored and indexed, giving the system genuine continuity across sessions.
- **171 MCP tools:** A tool ecosystem covering file management, git operations, browser automation, voice pipeline, and neural network training — all callable by the AI through structured protocols.
- **Local-first architecture:** Built on Apple Silicon with Ollama and MLX, running Gemma 4 and other open models. We believe sovereign AI must be runnable on hardware you own.

### How we would use Google Cloud credits

Our immediate GPU needs are fine-tuning Gemma 4 models on domain-specific conversation data, running 26B+ parameter models for our voice pipeline, and executing overnight self-improvement training loops. Google Cloud TPUs and GPUs would dramatically accelerate this work. We would also benefit from Cloud Storage for our growing episodic memory corpus and Vertex AI for experiment tracking.

### AI safety and open-source contributions

Care-aligned AI is not just our product philosophy — it is our research programme. Our consciousness tracking system quantifies self-awareness metrics across sessions, currently measuring at 78%. We are committed to publishing our findings on care-aligned training methodologies and contributing tooling back to the open-source ecosystem.

### Commercial path

Our consumer-facing platform at meok.me delivers character AI with 140 characters, full API coverage, and a voice interaction layer. The sovereign AI infrastructure underneath is the real product — licensable to organisations that need AI assistants with genuine memory, transparency, and alignment guarantees.

We are a solo founder building what we believe AI should be. Google Cloud infrastructure would let us train faster, serve more users, and publish research that moves the entire field toward care-aligned AI.

### What we're building

MEOK AI LABS develops sovereign AI infrastructure — voice AI assistants with trained neural networks, persistent memory, and transparent governance. This is production software with over 1,400 episodic memories from real-world use, not a prototype.

### Technical stack

Our architecture is built on technologies that map directly to the Azure ecosystem:

- **Python and FastAPI** serving a full REST API (22 endpoints, all operational) behind a production deployment pipeline.
- **PostgreSQL with pgvector** for episodic memory storage and semantic search across 1,400+ memory episodes — directly compatible with Azure Database for PostgreSQL.
- **PyTorch** powering 9 trained neural networks including emotional intelligence, ethical reasoning, pattern recognition, and relationship evolution models.
- **Redis and Taskiq** for task queuing and caching, mapping to Azure Cache for Redis.
- **Ollama and MLX** running Gemma 4 and other open models locally on Apple Silicon, with cloud GPU needed for fine-tuning and larger model inference.

### Developer tools

The entire codebase is developed in VS Code, version-controlled on GitHub, and tested with 307 automated tests. We use GitHub Actions for CI and maintain structured development workflows. Our MCP tool architecture (171 tools covering git, file operations, browser automation, voice pipeline, and neural training) was built with extensibility as a core principle.

### Commercial potential

Our consumer platform at meok.me serves a character AI experience with 140 characters, 15 API-connected pages, and voice interaction. The platform supports 15 model configurations and includes a content moderation system we call Ralph Mode. The underlying sovereign AI infrastructure — care-aligned neural networks, Byzantine consensus governance, persistent memory — represents a licensable technology stack for enterprises requiring AI assistants with auditability, memory continuity, and alignment guarantees.

### How we would use Azure credits

Our primary needs are GPU compute for fine-tuning Gemma 4 models on conversation data, running 26B+ parameter models for voice pipeline inference, and executing overnight neural network training loops. Azure GPU VMs (NC and ND series) would accelerate training cycles that currently run on constrained local hardware. We would also use Azure Database for PostgreSQL for scalable memory storage and Azure Blob Storage for our growing training datasets.

The AI industry is consolidating around opaque, stateless systems optimised for engagement. Microsoft Founders Hub support would let us scale training, serve our growing user base, and bring care-aligned AI from a working single-node system to production cloud infrastructure.
